Trailcrest

Trailcrest is the innovative game core that serves as the foundation to all our games. It is specifically designed to overcome some of the most common problems with educational software.

More Accurate Scoring.

Trailcrest uses an original algorithm for more accurate scoring. Unlike "raw percentage" scoring, which loses its reliability the less questions there are, the Trailcrest algorithm most closely represents the player's grasp of the material.

Intelligent Level Adjustment.

As play progresses, Trailcrest automatically adjusts the content difficulty to ensure the player is receiving the best material for them. When it detects that content is too hard, it will lower the level; when the player excels over time, the level is raised. Level lockout ensures the content is never too easy - when a player has proven mastery of a level, it can only be accessed by a manual teacher override.

Universal Save Files.

Our entire Operation SpyRat series uses universal save files. A player can create an agent profile in one game, and use that same profile in all other games and grade levels in the series. Common settings are applied globally, and educators can access scores across multiple subjects and grade levels with one click.

Automated Help System.

Trailcrest can detect when a player is having extra trouble with a concept, and will automatically provide help. We don't mean the vague hints seen in many games. Animated characters explain tough concepts in detail, while brief tutorial animations ensure full understanding of all of the game's activities.

Complete Teacher Control.

No computer is perfect, and none can read minds. With this in mind, Trailcrest gives educators the ultimate control over the game. Levels can be adjusted freely. Level adjustment parameters, auto help features, word lists...all of it is in the teacher's grasp. Changes can be applied to specific students, and teacher controls are password protected to prevent tampering.

What is Trailcrest's license?

Trailcrest is licensed under the GPL 3.0, so you can use it for any open-source project. We'll be switching to a slightly less restrictive open source license soon, to allow compatibility with permissive-licensed projects.

If you want to use Trailcrest in a closed-source project, we'll be offering a paid license upon release.

When will Trailcrest be ready?

Trailcrest will be in active development soon. We are building several pieces we'll be needing to create it.

We'd love your help! Check out our Developers page for details on how you can get involved.
